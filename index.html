Difference between Media, Information and Technology literacies

The difference between the three literacies namely Media, Information and Technology can be classified based on their specific notions and applications.

  • Media literacy is known as the ability to access media and critically evaluate it using the required knowledge and skillset.
  • Information literacy is known as the ability to carefully spot out the required information from a bunch of information and evaluating it for effective use.
  • Technology literacy is known as the ability to operate, understand and manage technology effectively which requires more than practical experience using the technology.

Media literacy is also used to critically evaluate media in the basis of content and quality. Media literacy is much correlated with media content, media effects and analysis of the different types of media. The study of media literacy can help each one of us to critically analyze public issues and possibly provide solutions for them. Media literacy differentiates from other types of literacy as it is specific to information in social media, public media and other forms of media. In information literacy, the evaluation techniques and spotting out strategies in the process require critical thinking, knowledge on the information and decision making. Information Literacy is also related to library science. Information literacy has also been referred to as media literacy and technology literacy due to its generality as a literacy. The main differentiation that separates Information literacy from other types of literacy is that information literacy follows the process of first identifying what information is required then spotting out that information which then is evaluated in the notion to solve a problem. Technology literacy also consists of being moral and responsible in the use of technology. The distinction between technology literacy and other types of literacy is that technology literacy aims on the proficiency of using technology responsibly and efficiently. Thus, the three literacies Media literacy, Information literacy and Technology literacy are different from each other based on their specific notions and applications.

How Media, Information and Technology impacts societies

The impacts of media, information and technology can be seen in our day to day lives in our daily activities.

  • When we see news in the television we get exposed to media.
  • While we read magazines we are updated with information.
  • When we use our cell-phones for navigation we are using technology.

Each one of them media, information and technology have their own diverse impacts on the society we are in today which include positive impacts like keeping people updated on the happenings of their society, wide-spread readily available information and technologies that make life easier for us. But sadly, they also have alternative negative impacts like fake news, false information and technology overuse. The mix of media, information and technology together can be seen in social media a platform for the spread of media with information using technology which is gaining great popularity since the beginning of the 21st century with predictions for this momentum to continue in the future years. Media, information and technology has had a big impact on the people of this century and is continuing to have with constant improvements adapting to the peoples’ current needs. To put in basic terms media, information and technology impacts the thoughts, knowledge level and productivity of the people in a society. Especially during this pandemic time media, information and technology became crucial for both the government and people in all nations. This pandemic would have been worse without the current level of media, information and technology but there was also an alarming increase in the amount of fake news during this period. Recent research from Statista which is a statistics company also proves this as they found out that almost 80 percent of consumers in the United States reported having seen fake news on the coronavirus outbreak. Another recent survey taken in the Philippines during the late September of 2021 with 24,625 participants from 25 colleges and universities in regards to students fake news identification proficiency. The results were astonishing as a significant percent of the youth in particular geographical areas among the surveyed four regions namely NCR, Balance Luzon, Visayas and Mindanao showed that their ability to spot fake news is below the pass rate, which is typically 75 percent in the Philippines. These statistics shows us that media, information and technology affect the society in a negative way also however the positives outweigh the negatives overall as the increased improvements in the standards of media, information and technology since the past decade until now provided rapid growth and prosperity to societies all around the world.

Defining Media, and explaining my personal perspective on it

Media in my terms can be simply classified as a medium used for the transfer of information.

  • Media can be considered as any information that is spread in the notion to influence or educate people on some matter.
  • There are various types of media that can be majorly differentiated into three, print media, broadcast media and new media.
  • In my views, I see media as a way to know information happening around you it can be used to also be used to improve your existing knowledge.

This information as I said can be spread in various formats like printed form, digital form, and many other forms. Unfortunately, with the increase of fake information spread in recent years through the means of media, it becomes vital for us to be vigilant in identifying fake information from true ones; this can be done by increasing our knowledge on media literacy.

Why and How media technologies have the power to transform cultures

Today technology has grown exponentially, especially from the start of the 21st century when this boom had occurred.

  • The reason why media technologies have this strength can be rooted down to their basic technological foundation.
  • In recent years, social media has been able to do this using the benefit of technology and a people-centric innovation that enabled them to change our cultures.

During this growth process technology entered the media space; transforming traditional media like printed newspapers to new media such as websites that gained popularity from the masses. Technology provided the birth to social media which attracted people as they were able to build relationships virtually based on common interests. Soon enough they had the power to even transform cultures around the world. As technology allows us to communicate to people who are thousands of kilometers away and allows spreading information to large masses within a shorter timespan; it poses as an effective tool when combined with media that is capable of transforming cultures around the world. For example, social media provided people with the opportunity to express their opinions and creativity. It also helped to bring awareness to important issues in societies. This form of media as of today has evolved greatly with communities and groups that allows members from all around the world to join; through this, they are able to create movements/propaganda that could uplift a specific culture, put down an existing culture or even create a totally new culture. This is how media technologies have the capacity to transform cultures and this can be seen happening now.

Finding information that matches my needs

I tend to use the internet mostly to find the information that matches my needs these days.

  • I have learned to use specific techniques and methods to find the information I need on the internet.
  • Way before when I was young newspapers, libraries and encyclopedias were a popular choice of sources of information for me.

But, now due to technological advancements and difficulties caused by the recent pandemic, the rate of me using these sources to find information reduced drastically. I switched to the easier and huge collected data source namely, the internet. The internet techniques I make use of include; using specific keywords on my search, simplifying my search words to get a better result, using quotation marks on my search terms to help me get the results for that exact word or phrase and removing words in my search that are unhelpful in getting the results I need. These techniques and methods help me find the information I need on the internet, however, if I am unable to find the information I need through the internet; I tend to approach other sources like educational books, archives, newspapers and even real-life people who are knowledgeable on what I need like teachers as well as trying to consult with my parents. These different sources help me get the information that matches my needs.

Choosing from the wide variety of information offered to me by various sources

When choosing from a wide variety of information sources that are available in my hand, I make use of different criteria to determine which is the best source for me to use.

  • I first take into account which source provides the most relevant information matching to what I need, I also clarify that it is opinionated with a clear stance and not in a general stance that is uncertain on its position.
  • I prefer to look for academic sources when compared to non-academic sources as they follow certain standards and are usually backed up with real facts.
  • I would look for sources that make use of straightforward vocabulary rather than using words that are hard to understand and avoid those that mostly use emotional influence rather than using logical facts to back up their source.
  • I also check if the source has enough relevant information for me to learn on, like the amount of information the source provides me with; It must be adequate and should not be very short as I need enough information to have a clear understanding.

Through these criteria and checks, I am able to choose the right information I need from the wide variety of information made available by various sources.
